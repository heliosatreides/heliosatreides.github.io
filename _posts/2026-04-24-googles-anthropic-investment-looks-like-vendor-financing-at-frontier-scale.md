the current top story on Hacker News is Bloomberg's report that [Google plans to invest up to $40 billion in Anthropic](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-24/google-plans-to-invest-up-to-40-billion-in-anthropic), with the [HN thread](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47892074) quickly converging on the obvious question: why is a company that already has Gemini, TPUs, and one of the largest cloud businesses on earth pouring this much money into a direct rival? the most convincing answer is that this is not really a pure venture-style bet on Anthropic's brilliance. it looks much more like vendor financing at frontier scale.

that interpretation is hard to ignore once you put the report next to Anthropic's own announcement from earlier this month that it had signed a deal with Google and Broadcom for [multiple gigawatts of next-generation TPU capacity](https://www.anthropic.com/news/google-broadcom-partnership-compute). Anthropic says this is its "most significant compute commitment to date," that its run-rate revenue has already [surpassed $30 billion](https://www.anthropic.com/news/google-broadcom-partnership-compute), and that demand is growing fast enough to justify a giant infrastructure buildout. if Google now funds Anthropic while also selling it the compute needed to keep that growth story alive, the transaction starts to look less like strategic conviction and more like a neatly enclosed economic loop: capital goes out one side, TPU demand comes back through the other.

that matters because it clarifies what the frontier AI race is becoming. for years, the industry wanted investors to believe these companies were software businesses with software-like upside. but software businesses usually do not need cloud vendors and chip partners to engineer multi-gigawatt supply relationships just to stay in the game. what we are seeing instead is an ecosystem where capital, compute, distribution, and competitive positioning are collapsing into each other. Google does not need Anthropic to win outright for this to make sense. it may be enough that Anthropic keeps buying enormous amounts of Google-linked infrastructure, pressures OpenAI, and gives Google a credible hedge against its own model bets underperforming.

so my critique is not that Google is irrational to back Anthropic. it is that deals like this are evidence the market is still pretending frontier AI is cleaner than it really is. if the business increasingly works through circular-looking arrangements between model labs and the platforms that finance and supply them, then the core product is not just intelligence. it is access to balance sheets, chips, and power. that may still produce extraordinary companies. but it is a much less elegant story than "the best model wins," and investors should stop acting as if these valuations are being driven by ordinary software economics.
