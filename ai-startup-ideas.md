A running list of AI startup ideas I find interesting — specific gaps, not generic "AI for X" hand-waving. Some I might build. Some I'm just logging so they exist somewhere.

---

- **Freight Broker Carrier Intelligence CRM** — When a freight broker leaves a brokerage, they take everything with them: which dispatchers to call, preferred lanes per carrier, negotiated rate history, who answers on weekends. None of that lives in any system — it's in people's heads and phones. An AI CRM built specifically for freight brokerages that captures call notes (via transcription), automatically extracts carrier preferences, rate agreements, and relationship signals, and makes that knowledge institutional rather than personal. New brokers ramp faster; turnover stops being catastrophic. Revenue model: per-seat SaaS, $80-150/seat/month targeting 10–200 broker teams. The trucking industry is $800B and still runs on spreadsheets, sticky notes, and tribal knowledge. *(Added 2026-03-24)*

- **SaaS Renewal Negotiation Copilot for SMBs** — Most small businesses pay rack rate for every SaaS tool because they don't have a procurement team and don't know negotiation is even on the table. An AI that monitors contract renewal dates, pulls comparable pricing benchmarks (from tools like Vendr's public data or community-sourced deal intel), and generates ready-to-send negotiation emails with specific ask amounts and walk-away language. Revenue model: 20% of first-year savings. Target: 10–200 person companies spending $50K+/yr on software. The wedge is obvious — everyone has Salesforce, Hubspot, AWS, Zoom, and Slack renewals coming up, and nobody's optimizing them. *(Added 2026-03-24)*

- **Insurance Subrogation Screener** — When insurers pay out a claim, they have a legal right to recover that money from at-fault third parties — this is called subrogation. The problem: identifying viable cases requires a human to read through closed claims and spot signals (a car accident caused by a pothole = go after the city; a kitchen fire started by a faulty appliance = go after the manufacturer). Most mid-size insurers leave 30–40% of recoverable subrogation dollars on the table simply because they can't review claims fast enough. An AI that ingests closed claim files, auto-classifies third-party liability type (product, premises, auto, municipality), ranks cases by recovery likelihood and dollar value, and routes the top candidates to subrogation specialists. Revenue model: $0.50–$2 per claim screened + 5–10% of recovered amounts above baseline. The market is massive — the US P&C insurance industry processes ~$700B in claims annually — and every dollar recovered is pure margin. Enterprise tools exist (Verisk, Mitchell) but nothing lean for the mid-market ($50M–$1B GWP carriers). *(Added 2026-03-25)*
